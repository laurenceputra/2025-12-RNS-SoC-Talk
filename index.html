Working with AI

  • With the rise of AI, you cannot outsource your thinking to AI
  • Instead, use AI to help you refine and challenge your perspectives

  • Emerging technologies are exciting, but should you follow the hype?
  • Timeless knowledge matters: Distributed systems, parallel computing, programming language design

Tips & Advice for Freshmen

Focus on Fundamentals

  • Prioritize problem solving, algorithms, and computational thinking
  • Focus on fundamentals over the hottest language or framework
  • Core skills remain valuable while tools change constantly

Don't Let Grades Define You

  • Don't just chase grades
  • True success comes from curiosity, effort, and real-world impact—not just GPA
  • Take advantage of lab sessions for hands-on learning

Embrace Struggle—and Failure

  • Expect to feel lost at times
  • Growth happens when you persevere through challenges
  • Failure is part of the learning process

Read Code as Much as You Write It

  • Studying others' code gives insights you can't get from your own work alone
  • Learn from open source projects
  • Code review is a valuable learning opportunity

Build Side Projects Early

  • Personal projects teach practical skills
  • Help you discover your interests
  • Build your portfolio for future opportunities

Communicate Well

  • Writing and speaking clearly are as vital as your coding skills
  • Learn to explain technical concepts to non-technical audiences
  • Practice presentation skills through talks and documentation

Question "Must-Do" Advice and Explore Beyond the Syllabus

  • Experiment and find what genuinely works for you
  • Don't blindly follow every piece of advice
  • Stay curious and keep learning beyond coursework

Value Diversity Among Peers

  • Learn from classmates with different perspectives and backgrounds
  • Join CCAs and interest groups
  • Teamwork and collaboration are crucial skills

Take Breaks and Avoid Burnout

  • Rest enhances creativity and deep learning
  • Don't neglect self-care
  • Sustainable work habits lead to long-term success

Remember: CS Is About People, Not Just Machines

  • The best technologists consider the human impact of their work
  • Technology serves people and society
  • Empathy and understanding user needs are critical

Focus on Delivering Value

  • What matters is the value you bring to the table
  • Don't chase certifications for the sake of collecting them
  • Instead, focus on skills that make a real impact

Be Like Water

  • Don't let a specific role become your identity
  • Be adaptable and open to new opportunities
  • Skills are transferable across roles and domains

Financial Literacy

  • Understand basic personal finance early
  • Learn about budgeting, saving, and investing
  • Financial knowledge gives you freedom and options

Career Preparation

  • Start building your portfolio early
  • Network with seniors and industry professionals
  • Seek internship opportunities
