What I Learned at SoC

Key skills and knowledge that benefited my career

CS is About Problem Solving

  • Your goal is not to learn to code
  • It's to learn how to solve problems from an engineering and scientific perspective
  • Computer Science is about managing complexity
  • Break down a problem into smaller problems, and continue breaking it down until you have many small problems you can easily solve

Technical Skills

  • Programming fundamentals
  • Data structures and algorithms
  • Software engineering practices

Why Fundamentals Matter

  • Core fundamental skills vs usage level skills
  • Frameworks and tools change constantly, but fundamentals remain
  • Strong foundations help you adapt quickly to new technologies
  • Deep understanding lets you debug and optimize effectively

Soft Skills

  • Problem-solving mindset
  • Teamwork and collaboration
  • Communication and presentation

Industry Insights & Trends

What freshmen should look out for

Emerging Technologies

  • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
  • Cloud Computing and DevOps
  • Cybersecurity

Industry Shifts

  • Remote work and distributed teams
  • Agile methodologies
  • Continuous learning culture

Globalisation & Competition

  • Globalisation is real, and making competition more intense
  • For us to have jobs in SG, we need to maintain our competitive advantage

Working with AI

  • With the rise of AI, you cannot outsource your thinking to AI
  • Instead, use AI to help you refine and challenge your perspectives

Choosing Your Path

  • Emerging technologies are exciting, but should you follow the hype?
  • Timeless knowledge matters: Distributed systems, parallel computing, programming language design
  • Do you want to follow people, or chart your own path?
  • AI may offer high salaries, but what is your conviction that it's the path for you?
  • Optimize for conviction, not for hype. Do things you won't regret

Tips & Advice for Freshmen

Fundamentals & Practice

  • Practice problem solving weekly
  • Master data structures & algorithms
  • Use office hours & study groups
Focus on a steady practice routine: 30-60 minutes daily for problem-solving and 1 project per month. Explain how fundamentals scale into system-level thinking.

Build & Document Projects

  • Ship small, public projects
  • Write tests & clear READMEs
  • Capture impact with metrics
Share a brief example: sample project, metric gained, and how you documented it in the README.

Career Prep & Internships

  • Yearly timeline: learn → build → apply
  • Start networking early
  • Practice mock interviews regularly
Recommend a weekly schedule for interview prep and a 6-9 month lead time for large company internship cycles.

Wellbeing & Finance

  • Budget & save early
  • Pace yourself; avoid burnout
  • Use campus resources (counselling)
Emphasize sustainable learning; a quick tip: schedule regular breaks and use campus resources before burnout becomes a crisis.
